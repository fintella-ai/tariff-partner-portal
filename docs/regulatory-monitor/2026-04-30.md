### 1. Euro-Notions Florida Now Lead IEEPA Refund Case (April 6-7, 2026)

**Source:** [International Trade Today - April 7, 2026](https://internationaltradetoday.com/news/2026/04/07/lead-ieepa-litigant-files-notice-of-dismissal-eaton-reissues-refund-order-in-new-case-2604070011), [Kelley Drye - Update: New CIT Case Takes the Lead](https://www.kelleydrye.com/viewpoints/blogs/trade-and-manufacturing-monitor/update-new-cit-case-takes-the-lead-for-ieepa-refunds)

**What happened:**
- Atmus Filtration, Inc. v. United States (No. 26-01259) was **voluntarily dismissed** April 6, 2026
- Senior Judge Richard K. Eaton immediately lifted the stay in **Euro-Notions Florida, Inc. v. United States** (CIT No. 1:25-cv-00595) and reissued the refund directive there on **April 7, 2026**

**Scope of April 7 Euro-Notions order (broader than CAPE Phase 1):**
- Unliquidated entries → CBP directed to liquidate without regard to IEEPA duties
- Liquidated entries where liquidation is **not final** → CBP directed to reliquidate without IEEPA duties
- Liquidated entries where liquidation **IS final** → CBP ALSO directed to reliquidate without IEEPA duties

This final point is critical: the court order covers even entries liquidated >180 days ago (protest window expired), beyond what CAPE Phase 1 operationalizes.

**Impact on calculator:**
- `checkEligibility()` was incorrectly labeling entries 81-180 days post-liquidation as "protest window expired" — **the protest window is 180 days, not 80 days**; the 80-day window is the CAPE Phase 1 window
- New three-tier logic added:
  - 0-80 days post-liquidation → `eligible` (CAPE Phase 1)
  - 81-180 days post-liquidation → `excluded_cape_protest_eligible` (file protest under 19 USC 1514)
  - >180 days post-liquidation → `excluded_expired` (litigation track via Euro-Notions order)
- A CBP status report on CAPE progress was due in Euro-Notions on April 28, 2026

### 2. Section 232 Restructuring — Proclamation 11021 (April 6, 2026) + HTS Technical Corrections (April 29, 2026)

**Sources:**
- [White House Fact Sheet - April 2, 2026](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-tariffs-on-steel-aluminum-and-copper-imports/)
- [Federal Register Doc. 2026-06960 - April 9, 2026](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/09/2026-06960/strengthening-actions-taken-to-adjust-imports-of-aluminum-steel-and-copper-into-the-united-states)
- **[FR Doc. 2026-08297 - April 29, 2026](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/29/2026-08297/notice-of-technical-corrections-to-the-harmonized-tariff-schedule-of-the-united-states-for-duties)** ← published yesterday; technical corrections to Annex IV of Proclamation 11021

**What changed (effective April 6, 2026 for new entries only):**

| What | Before (IEEPA era) | After (April 6, 2026+) |
|---|---|---|
| Steel HTS codes | 9903.80.01–9903.81.19 | 9903.82.* family |
| Aluminum HTS codes | 9903.85.01–9903.85.67 | 9903.82.* family |
| Copper HTS codes | 9903.78.* (investigation only) | 9903.82.* family |
| Rate basis | Metal content value | **Full customs value** |
| Pure metal/derivative rates | 25% flat | 50% pure / 25% derivative |
| De minimis | None | ≤15% metal content exempt |
| Copper Section 232 tariff | None (investigation pending) | **Yes — now covered** |

**Why this matters for the calculator:**
- The IEEPA refund period ended February 23, 2026 — the old Section 232 HTS codes (9903.80.*, 9903.85.*) remain the correct reference for IEEPA-era anti-stacking analysis
- **No retroactive impact on IEEPA refund calculations**
- However: Copper is now a Section 232 product for the first time, meaning for Section 122 period entries (Feb 24–Jul 24, 2026), copper qualifies for the Section 232 exemption under 9903.03.03 instead of only the Annex II exclusion
- The `section232Exemptions` data in the seed file has been updated with `proclamation11021Update` fields noting the new codes, rates, and effective dates for post-IEEPA entries
