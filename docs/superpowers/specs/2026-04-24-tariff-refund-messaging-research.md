## A. Messaging Patterns to Steal for Partner Landing

**1. Reframe client proof points as partner opportunity:**
- Client pitch: *"$166B in refunds, 330,000 eligible importers"* → Partner pitch: *"330,000 businesses need this help. Every one is a referral commission."*
- Client pitch: *"pay nothing until refund hits your account"* → Partner pitch: *"easiest referral you'll ever make — your client risks zero"*
- Client pitch: *"$22M/day in interest accruing"* → Partner pitch: *"the clock is literally costing your clients money every day you don't introduce them"*

**2. Market-size stats to cite (all citable):**
- *"$166 billion owed to 330,000 U.S. importers"* ([natlawreview](https://natlawreview.com/article/166-billion-question-what-importers-need-know-about-tariff-refund-process))
- *"Tariff refunds are America's hottest topic — NPR called the CBP portal 'America's hottest website.'"* ([npr.org](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/19/nx-s1-5786635/tariff-refunds-customs-ace-portal))
- *"Refunds accrue $22 million in interest per day."* ([natlawreview](https://natlawreview.com/article/166-billion-question-what-importers-need-know-about-tariff-refund-process))
- *"Typical importer recovery: $80K – $1M+ per client."* ([ayarlaw](https://ayarlaw.com/tariff-refund-resources/how-much-tariff-refund-can-i-get))

**3. Eligibility framing partners can use as a qualifier filter:**
*"If your client imported anything from overseas between Feb 4, 2025 and Feb 24, 2026 — furniture, electronics, auto parts, apparel, raw materials, anything — they are almost certainly owed money. That includes 'downstream' clients who didn't import directly but had tariff costs passed to them by their shipper."*

**4. Tone to match (Frost + competitors agree):**
- Authoritative but plainspoken — no legalese in hero
- Urgency-framed but not hype-y (real 180-day protest deadlines)
- *"Level the playing field"* / *"you don't have to do it alone"* David-vs-Goliath tone plays well with SMB importers

## C. UX Patterns to Copy

1. **Phone-first CTA repeated every section** — Frost and Ayar both lead with phone numbers, not forms. For partner landing: repeat the "apply to partner" button 3+ times on scroll.
2. **Dollar-anchored social proof band** — Quinn Emanuel ($12B, $175B), McCune ($23M/$26M/$200M verdict stack). Build a partner version: *"$166B market / 20% of firm fee / zero client risk."*
3. **4-step visible intake/process** — McCune shows 4-step intake; Ayar shows 4-factor calculator methodology. Mirror on partner landing: *Apply → Approved → Get your link → Earn on every referral.*
4. **Eligibility "am I qualified" spec box** — Ayar's cleanest pattern: concrete date window (Feb 4, 2025 – Feb 24, 2026), concrete exclusions (not Section 301/232), concrete importer-type requirement. Mirror as a partner-facing *"Is my client a fit?"* checklist — turns partners into self-qualifiers.
5. **Skip the calculator** — Ayar explicitly argues *"Online tariff refund calculators fall short"*. Don't build one; instead, make the partner's job a one-click intro, not a quoting tool.
6. **Urgency pegged to a real deadline, not fake scarcity** — *"180 days from liquidation"* and *"$22M/day in interest"* are the legit urgency levers. Use them verbatim.
7. **Credentialed spokesperson photo + bylines** — Seligman (Harvard/Yale/Stanford) on tariffrefundproject.com. Partner landing should feature Frost Law principal + press-mention logo bar (WSJ, Bloomberg, NYT all cover IEEPA refunds; Frost Law has direct placements).
8. **Contingency = zero-risk framing repeated** — every competitor hammers *"pay nothing until refund arrives"*. Partner landing should mirror it for the partner: *"You never handle paperwork. You never guarantee an outcome. You never invoice your client. You just make the intro."*
